Cattle and Buffaloe Diseases

Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD)

  • Cause: Foot and Mouth Disease Virus (FMDV)
  • Symptoms:
    • High fever (up to 106°F) lasting 2–3 days.
    • Blisters (vesicles) on the tongue, gums, lips, teats, and hooves.
    • Excessive salivation with a stringy, foamy appearance.
    • Lameness and reluctance to move due to painful hoof lesions.
    • Loss of appetite and reduced milk production.
    • Weight loss and weakness.
  • Transmission:
    • Direct contact with infected animals via saliva, milk, urine, feces, or semen.
    • Airborne transmission over long distances.
    • Contaminated equipment, feed, clothing, and vehicles.
  • Diagnosis: Laboratory tests (ELISA, PCR)
  • Treatment:
    • No specific antiviral treatment.
    • Soft, easily digestible food.
    • Disinfecting lesions with antiseptics (potassium permanganate or alum solution).
    • Antibiotics for secondary bacterial infections.
  • Prevention and Control:
    • Quarantine, disinfection of affected areas
    • Vaccination, movement restrictions

Bovine Tuberculosis

  • Cause: Mycobacterium bovis
  • Symptoms:
    • Chronic weight loss despite good appetite.
    • Chronic cough, especially after exercise.
    • Enlarged lymph nodes.
    • Low-grade fever, lethargy.
    • Reduced milk production.
    • In advanced cases, difficulty in breathing.
  • Transmission:
    • Aerosol (Airborne) Transmission: Inhalation of infected droplets from coughing animals.
    • Ingestion: Contaminated milk, feed, or water.
    • Direct Contact: Through open wounds or mucous membranes.
    • Vertical Transmission: From infected mother to calf via milk.
  • Diagnosis: Tuberculin skin test, blood tests
  • Treatment: Not usually treated; culling infected animals
  • Prevention and control:
    • Surveillance, controlled culling
    • Biosecurity measures, testing

Sheep & Goat Diseases

Blue Tongue

  • Cause: Bluetongue virus (BTV)
  • Symptoms:
    • Fever
    • Swelling of the tongue and lips
    • Difficulty breathing
    • Weight loss
  • Transmission: Biting midges
  • Diagnosis: PCR test, serological tests
  • Treatment: Supportive care
  • Prevention: Vaccination
  • Control: Vector control

Internal Parasites (Worms)

  • Cause: Various parasitic worms (e.g., roundworms, tapeworms)
  • Symptoms:
    • Weight loss
    • Diarrhea
    • Poor growth
    • Anemia
  • Transmission: Contaminated pasture, feces
  • Diagnosis: Fecal egg count
  • Treatment: Anthelmintics (dewormers)
  • Prevention: Rotational grazing, deworming

Pig Diseases

Swine Fever

  • Cause: Classical swine fever virus
  • Symptoms:
    • High fever
    • Skin hemorrhages
    • Loss of appetite
    • Weakness
  • Transmission: Direct contact, contaminated feed
  • Diagnosis: PCR test
  • Treatment: No cure; culling
  • Prevention: Biosecurity, vaccination

Porcine Reproductive and Respiratory Syndrome (PRRS)

  • Cause: PRRS virus
  • Symptoms:
    • Reproductive failure in sows
    • Respiratory distress in piglets
    • Poor growth rate
    • Increased mortality
  • Transmission: Direct contact, aerosol, contaminated equipment
  • Diagnosis: PCR test
  • Treatment: Supportive care
  • Prevention: Vaccination, biosecurity

Poultry Diseases

Avian Influenza (Bird Flu)

  • Cause: Avian influenza virus
  • Symptoms:
    • Respiratory distress
    • Swelling of the head
    • Decreased egg production
    • Sudden death
  • Transmission: Direct contact, contaminated feed/water
  • Diagnosis: PCR test
  • Treatment: No cure; supportive care
  • Prevention: Vaccination, biosecurity

Coccidiosis

  • Cause: Eimeria protozoa
  • Symptoms:
    • Bloody diarrhea
    • Weakness
    • Loss of appetite
    • Reduced growth rate
  • Transmission: Contaminated litter, feces
  • Diagnosis: Fecal examination
  • Treatment: Anticoccidial drugs
  • Prevention: Vaccination, proper sanitation
